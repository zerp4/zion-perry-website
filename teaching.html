Teaching

Teaching & mentorship

Whether I'm lecturing on protein structure, helping an undergraduate student submit their first cluster job, or teaching a high schooler how to pipette, I hope to communicate the wonder of scientific discovery through my teaching. At both MIT and Yale, I've had numerous opportunites to continually grow in my teaching practice, whether through giving a lecture on single molecule techniques, designing an online RNAfold activity, or leading a strawberry DNA extraction lab. More recently, I've been mentoring students on their own research projects and have been enjoying seeing them apply creative ideas.

Featured Course

Beautiful Biology: From DNA to Proteins

This was my favorite course to design - a hands-on introduction to computational and experimental molecular biology for high school students. Each student was assigned their own gene and analyzed it across the layers of DNA, RNA, and protein as the week progressed, culminating in final presentations.
